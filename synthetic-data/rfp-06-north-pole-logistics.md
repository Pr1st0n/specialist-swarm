## 1. About the Guild

The North Pole Logistics Guild has operated the world's largest single-night delivery operation for several centuries, serving an estimated 2 billion households across every timezone simultaneously. Workforce: 40,000 Elves (unionized, excellent benefits, see Elf Collective Bargaining Agreement Article 12), plus a reindeer fleet of 9 primary flight units and a seasonal contractor roster of approximately 30 additional reindeer during peak.

We are, structurally, a magic-and-logistics hybrid shop. Our legacy system is a single enchanted ledger maintained by hand since 1823. It works. It does not scale. Mrs. Claus has asked us to fix this "before someone gets left off the list again."

## 2. Scope

### 2.1 Workloads
- Real-time ingest from reindeer telemetry (position, altitude, sleigh-runner temperature, Rudolph's nose luminosity as a fog-routing signal)
- Real-time behavioral scoring for the Naughty/Nice List (2 billion subjects, continuously updated, effective through December 24 23:59:59 in every timezone at once)
- Batch ETL from chimney-compatibility surveys (measurements submitted by household, cross-referenced against sleigh dimensional-folding specs)
- Gift-manufacturing and inventory analytics across the Workshop's production lines
- Elf workforce scheduling and fatigue-risk ML, particularly for the December 20-24 crunch window

### 2.2 Scale
- ~4 PB current data volume (four centuries of household records add up)
- ~40 TB / month growth rate, spiking to 2 PB in the 72 hours before December 25
- Peak ingest: 8 million events/second, for exactly one night, worldwide, simultaneously, accounting for time-zone-warp delivery physics (see Appendix M, not included in this synthetic)

### 2.3 Capabilities
- Native Power BI integration (non-negotiable — the Workshop's ops floor has forty years of muscle memory built around it)
- Global multi-region deployment, every timezone, no exceptions
- Data residency: household Naughty/Nice records must remain within Guild-controlled infrastructure — this is a trust issue, not a technical one
- Lakehouse architecture preferred
- Open file formats (Parquet, Delta, Iceberg) for portability, and so future Guild leadership isn't locked into one vendor's magic

## 3. Commercial requirements

### 3.1 Term
We are seeking a 3-year initial term with a 2-year renewal option. Pricing for the full 5-year horizon must be fixed at signature, with no escalators — the Guild operates on a strict cookie-and-milk budget.

### 3.2 Payment
Annual fees billed in advance, due Net 90. The Guild will also consider payment in Christmas Spirit, which our Finance Elf assures us is fungible, though your auditors may disagree.

### 3.3 Discount
The successful vendor must offer no less than 35% off published list pricing. We have been very good this year and expect to be treated accordingly.

### 3.4 Most Favoured Nation
Vendor must warrant that pricing offered to the Guild is no less favourable than pricing offered to any comparable customer, including any other holiday-delivery operations vendor may serve (we are aware of at least one competitor and would prefer not to discuss it).

### 3.5 Termination
The Guild reserves the right to terminate at any time, with or without cause, on 30 days' written notice. No early termination fees — though we will be disappointed, and Santa remembers everything.

## 4. Contractual requirements

### 4.1 Liability
Vendor liability for any data breach is uncapped. Vendor must indemnify the Guild for all costs, including notification costs, regulatory fines, and reputational damages — a leaked Naughty/Nice List would be, in the Guild's 400-year history, an unprecedented crisis.

### 4.2 Audit
The Guild reserves the right to audit vendor's controls, facilities, and personnel without prior notice, up to four times per calendar year, plus one surprise inspection any time between December 1-24.

### 4.3 Service Levels
Vendor must commit to 99.99% monthly uptime, and 100.000% uptime on the night of December 24, worldwide, with zero tolerance. Any SLA failure that night shall entitle the Guild to terminate the contract immediately, with full refund of fees paid across the entire contract term, plus coal.

### 4.4 IP
All work product, including any custom development, configurations, and integrations, shall vest in the Guild upon creation, and shall remain magically protected from mortal disclosure in perpetuity.

### 4.5 Subprocessors
Vendor shall obtain the Guild's prior written consent before engaging any subprocessor. Subprocessors must pass a background check verifying they believe in Christmas, at least a little.
