# Request for Proposal — Galactic Trade Federation, Sector 9 Procurement Directorate Omni-Species Data Platform

**From:** Galactic Trade Federation, Sector 9 Procurement Directorate (procurement@sector9federation-synthetic.example)
**To:** Vendor — BTS-Synthetic
**Issued:** Cycle 4471.203, Standard Galactic Calendar
**Response due:** Cycle 4471.217 (14 standard cycles)
**Award expected:** Cycle 4471.260, pending ratification by the Sector 9 Assembly (all 340 member species must be quorate)

## 1. About the Federation

The Galactic Trade Federation, Sector 9 Directorate, coordinates commerce, logistics, and diplomatic data-sharing across 340 member species spanning 1,900 inhabited systems. Combined Sector GDP ~1.4 quadrillion Federation Credits (Cycle 4470). Directorate headcount: 7,200 sapients (and 3 non-sapient administrative constructs who, per Assembly ruling 88-B, still count for headcount purposes).

We operate primarily on the Federation Standard Substrate ("cloud," in the parlance of newer member species, most recently your own). Legacy infrastructure includes several pre-Federation species' data systems still being decommissioned following the Sector's unification 40 cycles ago — including, notably, a species whose entire historical archive is stored as smell.

## 2. Scope

### 2.1 Workloads
- Real-time ingest from ~40,000 sensor arrays across member systems (including several sensor types that operate outside your species' currently known electromagnetic spectrum — see Appendix Q, not included in this synthetic)
- Batch ETL from 30+ member-species trade registries, several using non-decimal, non-linear, or non-chronological data structures
- BI and reporting for ~600 Sector analysts and Assembly delegates
- Self-service data preparation for ~150 cross-species data engineers, requiring real-time translation across 340 official languages
- Predictive trade-disruption ML for early warning of supply-chain issues across hyperspace lanes (planned, not active — the Assembly's Ethics Committee is still debating whether predictive models constitute "soft prophecy," a banned practice under three member species' religious codes)

### 2.2 Scale
- ~280 zettabytes current data volume
- ~12 zettabytes/month growth rate
- Peak ingest: 80 million events/second during Grand Trade Cycle openings, when all 1,900 systems transact simultaneously

### 2.3 Capabilities
- Native integration with the Assembly's existing holographic reporting interface (non-negotiable — 600 delegates across 340 species have exactly one UI they've all agreed to learn, and reopening that negotiation would take another 40 cycles)
- Multi-galaxy deployment: primary in the Sector 9 Core Worlds, secondary in the Outer Reach colonies, with acceptable latency defined in Appendix R (light-lag adjusted)
- Data residency: member-species sovereign data must remain within that species' designated systems per the Sovereignty Accords — this is the single most litigated clause in Federation contracting history
- Lakehouse architecture preferred (translated across 340 languages, this concept has caused at least one minor diplomatic incident, but the Assembly remains fond of it)
- Open file formats (Parquet, Delta, Iceberg) for portability, so no single member species' vendor can hold Federation data hostage during a trade dispute

## 3. Commercial requirements

### 3.1 Term
We are seeking a 3-year initial term (Standard Galactic Cycles) with a 2-cycle renewal option. Pricing for the full 5-cycle horizon must be fixed at signature, with no escalators, regardless of currency fluctuation across member-species economies.

### 3.2 Payment
Annual fees billed in advance, due Net 90 standard cycles, payable in Federation Credits, rare isotopes, or a mutually agreeable trade concession (subject to Assembly approval).

### 3.3 Discount
The successful vendor must offer no less than 35% off published list pricing, consistent with the Federation's Most-Favoured-Species trade doctrine, which predates and supersedes most vendor pricing models.

### 3.4 Most Favoured Nation
Vendor must warrant that pricing offered to the Federation is no less favourable than pricing offered to any comparable customer for the duration of the contract — "comparable" to be interpreted across all known galaxies, not merely the vendor's home system.

### 3.5 Termination
The Federation reserves the right to terminate at any time, with or without cause, on 30 standard cycles' written notice. No early termination fees or penalties, in keeping with the Non-Coercion Principle enshrined in the Sector 9 Charter.

## 4. Contractual requirements

### 4.1 Liability
Vendor liability for any data breach is uncapped and extends across all 340 member-species' legal frameworks simultaneously. Vendor must indemnify the Federation for all costs, including notification costs (delivered in 340 languages, several requiring physical rather than written delivery), regulatory fines, and reputational damages.

### 4.2 Audit
The Federation reserves the right to audit vendor's controls, facilities, and personnel without prior notice, up to four times per calendar cycle, using an Assembly-certified Multi-Species Audit Team. Audit costs shall be borne by the vendor, including any required atmospheric or gravitational accommodations for the audit team.

### 4.3 Service Levels
Vendor must commit to 99.99% monthly uptime. Any SLA failure during a Grand Trade Cycle opening shall entitle the Federation to terminate the contract immediately, with full refund of fees paid in the affected cycle, and formal notation in the vendor's Sector-wide trade record.

### 4.4 IP
All work product, including any custom development, configurations, and integrations created in the course of this engagement, shall vest in the Federation upon creation, in accordance with Assembly Ruling 12-A on cross-species intellectual sovereignty.

### 4.5 Subprocessors
Vendor shall obtain the Federation's prior written consent before engaging any subprocessor for any part of the services, including subprocessors based in systems outside current Federation jurisdiction.
