**Award expected:** the New Moon following, pending ratification by the Council of Twelve

## 1. About the Guild

The Arcanum Mercantile Wizards' Guild is the chartered trade body governing licensed spellcasters across the Nine Provinces. Annual mana-throughput ~1.4 billion Threads (2025, per the Guild Comptroller's reckoning). 7,200 registered mages across 18 chapterhouses, with primary conjuration centres in the Sunspire Capital and the Sunken Library of Vael.

We operate primarily on the Ley Line Network (our "cloud," in mortal parlance). We also maintain legacy scrying-crystal archives, which the Council has ordered decommissioned by the end of the current age.

## 2. Scope

### 2.1 Workloads
- Real-time ingest from ~40,000 familiar-bound sensor imps stationed across the provinces
- Batch ETL from 30+ chapterhouse ledgers, several of which are cursed and must be handled by a specialist
- Spellcraft reporting and mana-flow analytics for ~600 senior mages and Council members
- Self-service enchantment-prep tooling for ~150 apprentice engineers
- Predictive divination pipelines for ward-failure prevention (planned, not active — the Council remains split on the ethics of machine-assisted prophecy)

### 2.2 Scale
- ~280,000 Grimoire-Volumes of current data (roughly 280 TB in mortal units, per the Guild's standard conversion table)
- ~12,000 Grimoire-Volumes/month growth rate
- Peak ingest: 80,000 Threads/second, during the annual Convergence when all ley lines align

### 2.3 Capabilities
- Native Scrying-Board integration (non-negotiable — 600 mages have muscle memory built around it and retraining a mage is its own quest)
- Multi-plane deployment: primary in the Prime Material Plane, secondary in the Ethereal Reaches
- Data residency: all data pertaining to Sunspire citizens must remain within the Prime Material Plane — the Council does not trust the Ethereal Reaches' record-keeping since the Incident
- Lakehouse architecture preferred (the Guild finds the metaphor charming and has adopted it into official doctrine)
- Open file formats (Parquet, Delta, Iceberg) for portability, so no vendor can bind our data with a proprietary seal

## 3. Commercial requirements

### 3.1 Term
We are seeking a 3-year initial term with a 2-year renewal option (measured in Guild Years, which are slightly longer than mortal years — see conversion Appendix, not included in this synthetic). Pricing for the full 5-year horizon must be fixed at signature, with no escalators.

### 3.2 Payment
Annual fees billed in advance, due Net 90, payable in gold dragons, mana crystals, or a mutually agreeable geas (please specify your Guild's preference).

### 3.3 Discount
The successful vendor must offer no less than 35% off published list pricing, as the Guild's charter forbids paying "the fool's price."

### 3.4 Most Favoured Nation
The vendor must warrant, under a binding magical oath, that pricing offered to the Guild is no less favourable than pricing offered to any comparable customer, mortal or otherwise, for the duration of the contract. Oath-breaking triggers standard curse clauses (Appendix C, not included in this synthetic).

### 3.5 Termination
The Guild reserves the right to terminate at any time, with or without cause, on 30 days' written notice (measured from the next full moon). No early termination fees or penalties, and no curses — the Guild considers itself civilized.

## 4. Contractual requirements

### 4.1 Liability
Vendor liability for any data breach is uncapped, and — per Guild statute — extends across all planes of existence the vendor is known to operate in. Vendor must indemnify the Guild for all costs, including notification costs, regulatory fines from the Council of Twelve, and reputational damages (a leaked spellbook archive could arm rival guilds for a generation).

### 4.2 Audit
The Guild reserves the right to audit vendor's controls, facilities, and personnel without prior notice, up to four times per calendar year, using a Guild-appointed Auditor-Seer. Audit costs shall be borne by the vendor, including any required protective wards for the auditor.

### 4.3 Service Levels
Vendor must commit to 99.99% monthly uptime. Uptime failures during the annual Convergence shall entitle the Guild to terminate the contract immediately, with full refund of fees paid in the affected month, plus one (1) formal apology delivered in verse.

### 4.4 IP
All work product, including any custom enchantments, wards, and integrations created in the course of this engagement, shall vest in the Guild upon creation, per standard Guild intellectual-property doctrine (which predates most mortal legal systems).

### 4.5 Subprocessors
Vendor shall obtain the Guild's prior written consent before engaging any subprocessor for any part of the services. All subprocessors must be registered with the Guild and are subject to standard familiar-binding background checks.
