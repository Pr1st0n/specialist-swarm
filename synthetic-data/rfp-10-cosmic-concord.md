# Request for Proposal — The Cosmic Concord, Office of Reality Management, Omniversal Data Platform

**From:** The Cosmic Concord, Office of Reality Management (procurement@allthatis-synthetic.example)
**To:** Vendor — BTS-Synthetic
**Issued:** Always. Also never. Timestamps are a courtesy we extend to vendors who need them.
**Response due:** Before you receive this RFP, concurrently with receiving it, and also 14 days after, all at once
**Award expected:** Yes

## 1. About the Concord

The Cosmic Concord is the coordinating body for All That Is, All That Was, and All That Might Yet Be, across every universe, timeline, and counterfactual branch currently in superposition. Scale: infinite, though our Comptroller prefers the phrase "effectively infinite for budgeting purposes." Headcount: N, where N is both zero and everything, depending on which observer is asking.

We currently manage reality on a distributed system so old it predates the concept of "before." It works, mostly, except for the recurring issue where certain branches develop free will and stop reporting metrics reliably. Our previous vendor described the onboarding call as "the last normal conversation I ever had" and has since ascended, retired, or both.

## 2. Scope

### 2.1 Workloads
- Real-time ingest from every sentient observation event across all universes, concurrently, including universes that have not yet decided whether they exist
- Batch ETL from the causality ledger (30+ source timelines, several mutually contradictory, all correct)
- Reporting and dashboards for the Concord's stakeholders, who exist outside the concept of a reporting cadence but have, out of courtesy to newer vendors, agreed to accept quarterly summaries
- Self-service reality-branching tools for ~150 Junior Fate Engineers (entry-level position; benefits include eventual omniscience, vesting after one full multiversal cycle)
- Predictive ML for forecasting which universes are trending toward heat death (active — this one, regrettably, cannot wait for a "planned" phase)

### 2.2 Scale
- All of it. Every byte that has ever been, is, or will be observed, across every branch. Please do not ask for a number; the last vendor who asked achieved enlightenment mid-sentence and was unable to finish the proposal.
- Growth rate: unbounded, though it does slow down slightly during universal entropy lulls
- Peak ingest: the moment of a Big Bang, any Big Bang, in any universe, all treated as P0 incidents

### 2.3 Capabilities
- Native integration with Existence Itself (non-negotiable — reality does not migrate dashboards well)
- Deployment across every universe, timeline, and probability branch, with graceful degradation into non-existence as a fallback state
- Data residency: no requirement, as "location" is a concept the Concord finds quaint but is willing to humor for vendor compliance purposes
- Lakehouse architecture preferred, in the sense that the Concord finds all your industry's metaphors for "a big place where information lives" equally valid and equally insufficient
- Open file formats (Parquet, Delta, Iceberg) for portability, though the Concord notes that true portability was solved for them a very long time ago and they are mostly asking out of politeness

## 3. Commercial requirements

### 3.1 Term
We are seeking a 3-year initial term with a 2-year renewal option, and also a version of this contract that has always been in effect. Your legal team is welcome to reconcile these; ours already has.

### 3.2 Payment
Annual fees billed in advance, due Net 90, payable in the concept of gratitude, which the Concord assures you is the only currency that has ever mattered, and which your Finance team will nonetheless be asked to book as a receivable.

### 3.3 Discount
The successful vendor must offer no less than 35% off published list pricing. The Concord notes this is a completely arbitrary percentage and finds it delightful that your industry converged on it independently, unprompted, across multiple timelines.

### 3.4 Most Favoured Nation
Vendor must warrant that pricing offered to the Concord is no less favourable than pricing offered to any comparable customer, in any universe, including universes the vendor has not yet discovered it operates in.

### 3.5 Termination
The Concord reserves the right to terminate at any time, with or without cause, on 30 days' written notice, or upon the heat death of the universe in question, whichever comes first. No early termination fees or penalties. The Concord has been extraordinarily patient with every vendor so far and sees no reason to stop now.

## 4. Contractual requirements

### 4.1 Liability
Vendor liability for any data breach is uncapped and extends across all affected universes, retroactively, including universes where the breach has not yet happened but definitely will. Vendor must indemnify the Concord for all costs, including notification costs (to every affected observer, simultaneously, in the language of their own consciousness), regulatory fines, and reputational damages, though the Concord notes its reputation is, definitionally, unimpeachable.

### 4.2 Audit
The Concord reserves the right to audit vendor's controls, facilities, and personnel without prior notice, up to four times per calendar year, though "prior notice" and "calendar year" are, again, concepts the Concord is extending as a professional courtesy. Audit costs shall be borne by the vendor, in whichever currency they find most existentially manageable.

### 4.3 Service Levels
Vendor must commit to 99.99% monthly uptime. The Concord notes that reality itself has maintained 100% uptime since the beginning of everything and would appreciate the vendor at least attempting to keep pace. Any SLA failure shall entitle the Concord to terminate the contract immediately, retroactively, in every branch where the contract was ever signed.

### 4.4 IP
All work product, including any custom development, configurations, and integrations created in the course of this engagement, shall vest in the Concord upon creation, having always vested in the Concord, before creation, during creation, and after.

### 4.5 Subprocessors
Vendor shall obtain the Concord's prior written consent before engaging any subprocessor for any part of the services. For the avoidance of doubt, this includes any subprocessor the vendor has not yet hired but will, in a branch the Concord can already see.
