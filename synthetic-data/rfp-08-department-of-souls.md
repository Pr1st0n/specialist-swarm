## 1. About the Department

The Department of Souls & Ledgers is the administrative arm of the Sovereign Court of the Sunless Realm, responsible for intake processing, eternal-record reconciliation, and afterlife placement for all arriving souls. Current throughput ~1.4 billion souls under active management, with roughly 108,000 new arrivals per day (subject to Overworld mortality trends, which our Actuarial Wing tracks closely).

We operate what the living would call "legacy infrastructure" — the Great Ledger has been maintained continuously since before written language existed. It is, by any modern standard, due for a platform migration. The last vendor who attempted this did not survive the engagement, professionally speaking.

## 2. Scope

### 2.1 Workloads
- Real-time ingest from soul-intake checkpoints across all seven Circles
- Batch reconciliation of Karmic Ledger entries against lifetime conduct records (30+ source registries, several in dead languages)
- Reporting for the Court's quarterly Judgment sessions
- Self-service record lookup for ~150 Ledger Clerks (a notoriously understaffed department — recruiting is difficult, for reasons)
- Predictive reincarnation-routing ML (planned, not active — the Ethics Tribunal has concerns about "algorithmic fate")

### 2.2 Scale
- ~280 exabytes of current data volume, spanning all of recorded and unrecorded history
- ~12 exabytes/month growth rate
- Peak ingest: 80,000 souls/second during any large-scale Overworld mortality event

### 2.3 Capabilities
- Native integration with the Court's existing Scrying-Ledger interface (non-negotiable — the Judges will not learn a new tool, and you do not want to be the vendor who suggested it)
- Multi-realm deployment: primary in the Sunless Realm, secondary in the Grey Waiting Halls
- Data residency: soul records must never be replicated to Overworld infrastructure, under any circumstances, per the Concordat of the First Age
- Lakehouse architecture preferred, though the Department notes with interest that "lake of fire" was our first choice of name before your industry got there
- Open file formats (Parquet, Delta, Iceberg) for portability, so souls are never bound to a single vendor's proprietary afterlife

## 3. Commercial requirements

### 3.1 Term
We are seeking a 3-year initial term with a 2-year renewal option, though the Department notes that "eternal" is also an option we would consider for the right vendor.

### 3.2 Payment
Annual fees billed in advance, due Net 90. The Department does not deal in mortal currency; payment shall be rendered in Favors, Memories, or a small number of pre-approved Souls (see Schedule of Acceptable Considerations, not included in this synthetic — please consult Legal before agreeing to this line item).

### 3.3 Discount
The successful vendor must offer no less than 35% off published list pricing. The Department has eternity to negotiate and no particular urgency about doing so quickly.

### 3.4 Most Favoured Nation
Vendor must warrant that pricing offered to the Department is no less favourable than pricing offered to any comparable customer for the duration of the contract, which — per Section 3.1 — may be forever.

### 3.5 Termination
The Department reserves the right to terminate at any time, with or without cause, on 30 days' written notice. No early termination fees or penalties, and — the Department wishes to clarify, for vendor peace of mind — no actual curses either. That is not how this works. Please stop asking.

## 4. Contractual requirements

### 4.1 Liability
Vendor liability for any data breach is uncapped and extends across every reincarnation cycle affected. Vendor must indemnify the Department for all costs, including notification costs (to the affected souls directly, individually, which is logistically difficult), regulatory fines from the Court, and reputational damages.

### 4.2 Audit
The Department reserves the right to audit vendor's controls, facilities, and personnel without prior notice, up to four times per calendar year, using an Auditor drawn from the Court's own ranks. Audit costs shall be borne by the vendor, and the auditor should not be offered food or drink during the visit, per standard interplanar protocol.

### 4.3 Service Levels
Vendor must commit to 99.99% monthly uptime, measured in eternities. Any SLA failure during a Judgment session shall entitle the Department to terminate the contract immediately, with full refund of fees paid across all affected reincarnation cycles.

### 4.4 IP
All work product, including any custom development, configurations, and integrations, shall vest in the Department upon creation, and shall be recorded in the Great Ledger in perpetuity, which — the Department notes — is a considerably more durable form of IP protection than anything your legal team can offer.

### 4.5 Subprocessors
Vendor shall obtain the Department's prior written consent before engaging any subprocessor. Subprocessors are subject to standard soul-handling certification, which very few Overworld contractors currently hold.
